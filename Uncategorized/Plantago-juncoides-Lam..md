## Introduction
Plantago is a genus of about 200 species of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae, commonly called plantains or fleaworts. The common name plantain is shared with the unrelated cooking plantain. Most are herbaceous plants, though a few are subshrubs growing to 60 centimetres (24 inches) tall.

## Care Guide
Rush Plantain thrives in full sun and consistently moist to wet, even saline, soil conditions, emulating its native coastal salt marsh habitat. This hardy plant requires low to moderate fertilization, preferring well-drained yet consistently hydrated environments to flourish.

### Fertilizer
Low to Moderate. Adaptable to nutrient-poor or saline soils; a light application of a balanced, slow-release fertilizer once annually in spring is generally sufficient, or none if soil is naturally rich.
