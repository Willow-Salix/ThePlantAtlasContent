## Introduction
Moneses uniflora, the one-flowered wintergreen (British Isles), single delight, wax-flower, shy maiden, star of Bethlehem (Aleutians), St. Olaf's candlestick (Norway), wood nymph, or frog's reading lamp, is a plant of the family of  Ericaceae, that is indigenous to moist coniferous forests in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere from Spain to Japan and across North America. It is the sole member of genus Moneses.

## Care Guide
This forest floor plant thrives in cool, shaded conditions with consistently moist, well-drained soil. Protect it from direct sunlight and ensure adequate humidity. Fertilizer needs are minimal, mirroring its natural habitat with rich organic matter but low nutrient availability.

### Fertilizer
Light feeding, if any, with a balanced or acid-loving fertilizer, or a slow-release organic amendment. Heavy fertilization is generally not needed and can be detrimental.
