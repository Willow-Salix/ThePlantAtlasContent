## ðŸŒ± Overview
Pearl millet (Cenchrus americanus, commonly known as the synonym Pennisetum  glaucum) is the most widely grown type of millet. It has been grown in Africa and the Indian subcontinent since prehistoric times. The center of diversity and suggested area of domestication for the crop is in the Sahel zone of West Africa.

## ðŸ§¤ Care Instructions
Pearl Millet thrives in full sun and is highly drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Provide deep, infrequent watering once established, increasing frequency only during active growth or extreme heat. It benefits from light to moderate fertilization, especially if grown for grain production, but can tolerate less fertile soils.
