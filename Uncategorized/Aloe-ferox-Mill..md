## Introduction
Aloe ferox, commonly known as bitter aloe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asphodelaceae. This woody aloe is indigenous to southern Africa. 
It is one of several Aloe species used to make bitter aloes, a purgative medication, and also yields a non-bitter gel that can be used in cosmetics.

## Care Guide
Cape aloe thrives in bright, direct sunlight and requires minimal watering, allowing its soil to dry out thoroughly between applications. Mimicking its native arid to semi-arid scrubland habitat, it prefers lean soil and infrequent feeding, making it a low-maintenance succulent suitable for dry, sunny conditions.

### Fertilizer
Minimal fertilization; use a diluted, balanced succulent fertilizer once or twice during the active growing season (spring/summer) in lean soils.
