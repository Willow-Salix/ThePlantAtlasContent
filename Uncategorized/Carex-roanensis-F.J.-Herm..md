## Care Guide
The Roan Mountain Sedge requires cool, consistently moist to wet conditions, performing best in full sun to partial shade. It thrives with ample water, mimicking its native stream-side and seep habitats, and prefers minimal fertilization due to its adaptation to lean, high-elevation soils.

Low; it is adapted to lean, rocky soils. A light application of a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring is sufficient, or none if planted in a rich, organic medium.
