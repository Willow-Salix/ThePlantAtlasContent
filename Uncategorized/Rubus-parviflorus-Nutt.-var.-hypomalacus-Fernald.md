## Introduction
Rubus parviflorus, the fruit of which is commonly called the thimbleberry or redcap, is a species of Rubus with large hairy leaves and no thorns.
The species is native to northern temperate regions of North America. It produces red aggregate fruit similar in appearance to a raspberry; although edible, it is too soft for major commerce. It is cultivated as an ornamental.

## Care Guide
Thimbleberry thrives in cool, moist temperate climates, preferring partial sun to partial shade. It requires consistently moist, well-drained soil and benefits from a low to moderate annual feeding with organic matter to mimic its natural forest edge habitat.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; benefits from annual application of organic matter or a balanced slow-release fertilizer in spring, typical of forest edge soils.
