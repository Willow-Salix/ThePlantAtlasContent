## Introduction
Prosartes trachycarpa, the roughfruit fairybells,  rough-fruited fairybells or rough-fruited mandarin, is a North American species of plants in the lily family. The species is widespread, known from British Columbia to Ontario and south to Arizona and New Mexico. One isolated population was reported from Isle Royale in Lake Superior.

## Care Guide
This woodland plant thrives in part to full shade, requiring consistently moist, well-drained soil that is rich in organic matter. Provide regular watering to maintain soil moisture and a moderate feeding in spring to support healthy growth, mimicking its native temperate forest environment.

### Fertilizer
Moderate. Benefits from soil enriched with organic matter like compost, or a balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer applied in spring to support its growth in richer forest floor conditions.
