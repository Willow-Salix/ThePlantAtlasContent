## Introduction
Prunus fasciculata, also known as wild almond, desert almond, or desert peach is a spiny and woody shrub producing wild almonds, which is native to western deserts of North America.

## Care Guide
This desert-adapted shrub requires full sun and excellent drainage, thriving with minimal intervention once established. It is highly drought-tolerant, reflecting its arid native climate, and generally does not need fertilization. Plant in well-draining soil and water deeply but infrequently.

### Fertilizer
Generally not required. Thrives in lean soils; if growth is very poor, a light, balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring may be used sparingly.
