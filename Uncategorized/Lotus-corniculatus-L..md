## Introduction
Lotus corniculatus is a flowering plant in the pea family Fabaceae. Common names include common bird's-foot trefoil, eggs and bacon, birdsfoot deervetch, and just bird's-foot trefoil (a name also often applied to other Lotus spp.). It has a wide distribution and is a favored forage for livestock.

## Care Guide
Bird's-Foot Trefoil is a hardy legume best suited for full to partial sun in temperate climates, requiring moderate watering but tolerating drought once established. Its natural ability to fix nitrogen minimizes the need for external fertilization, making it a low-maintenance choice for various landscapes.

### Fertilizer
Low to no nitrogen fertilizer is required due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities as a legume. If soil is extremely poor, a balanced fertilizer with low nitrogen, or higher in phosphorus and potassium, can be applied sparingly.
