## Introduction
Centaurea iberica, the Iberian knapweed or Iberian star-thistle, is a species of Centaurea.  It is native to southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia. It is known elsewhere as an introduced species and a noxious weed.

## Care Guide
Iberian knapweed is a robust plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, characteristic of its native Mediterranean and semi-arid regions. It is exceptionally drought-tolerant once established, requiring infrequent watering to avoid root rot. Minimal fertilization is needed, as it is adapted to less fertile conditions.

### Fertilizer
Minimal to low fertilization; adapted to poor to average, well-draining soils. Over-fertilization is generally not recommended.
