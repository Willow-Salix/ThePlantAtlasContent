## Introduction
Lablab purpureus is a species of bean in the family Fabaceae. It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and it is cultivated throughout the tropics for food. English language common names include hyacinth bean, lablab-bean bonavist bean/pea, dolichos bean, seim or sem bean, lablab bean, Egyptian kidney bean, Indian bean, bataw and Australian pea. Lablab is a monotypic genus.

## Care Guide
This resilient tropical legume thrives in full sun and requires consistent moisture, particularly during its active growth and fruiting stages. While it naturally enriches soil with nitrogen, supplemental balanced fertilizer can boost yields. Ensure well-draining soil to prevent waterlogging.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate fertilization is generally sufficient. As a legume, it fixes its own nitrogen; therefore, excessive nitrogen should be avoided. A balanced NPK fertilizer can be applied if soil nutrients are poor, especially for phosphorus and potassium to support flowering and fruiting.
