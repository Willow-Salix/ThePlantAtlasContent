## Introduction
Salix exigua (sandbar willow, narrowleaf willow, or coyote willow; syn. S. argophylla, S. hindsiana, S. interior, S. linearifolia, S. luteosericea, S. malacophylla, S. nevadensis, and  S. parishiana) is a species of willow native to most of North America except for the southeast and far north, occurring from Alaska east to New Brunswick, and south to northern Mexico. It is considered a threatened species in Massachusetts while in Connecticut, Maryland, and New Hampshire it is considered endangered.

## Care Guide
This willow thrives in full sun and consistently moist to wet soil, mimicking its native riparian habitat. It is a robust plant that doesn't demand extensive fertilization, especially once established. Ensure adequate water to prevent stress, particularly in drier periods.

### Fertilizer
Generally low to moderate. In rich riparian soils, little to no supplemental fertilizer may be needed. For less fertile sites, a balanced slow-release fertilizer in spring can be beneficial for young plants.
