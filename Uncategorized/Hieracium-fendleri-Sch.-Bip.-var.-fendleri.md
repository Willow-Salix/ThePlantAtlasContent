## Introduction
Pilosella caespitosa (synonym Hieracium caespitosum, commonly known as meadow hawkweed, yellow hawkweed,
field hawkweed, king devil, yellow paintbrush, devil's paintbrush, yellow devil, yellow fox-and-cubs, and yellow king-devil)
is like several other Pilosella species and has a similar appearance to many of the hawkweeds.

## Care Guide
This plant thrives in sunny to partly shaded locations with consistent, moderate moisture and well-drained soil, mimicking its native montane habitats. It requires minimal fertilization, benefiting most from good soil structure and occasional light feeding. Ensure good drainage to prevent waterlogging.

### Fertilizer
Light to moderate feeding. A balanced, slow-release fertilizer or organic matter can be applied sparingly, especially in spring, as montane soils are not excessively rich.
