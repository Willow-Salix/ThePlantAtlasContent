## Introduction
Dicranum is a genus of mosses, also called wind-blown mosses or fork mosses. 
These mosses form in densely packed clumps. Stems may fork, but do not branch. In general, upright stems will be single but packed together. Dicranum is distributed globally. In North America these are commonly found in Jack pine or Red pine stands.

## Care Guide
Strict Broom Moss thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, emulating its native forest floor habitat. Ensure constant moisture for the substrate and high ambient humidity, while strictly protecting it from any direct sunlight. Fertilization is rarely needed due to its low nutrient requirements.

### Fertilizer
Very low. Mosses obtain most nutrients from water and air. If used at all, apply a highly diluted liquid fertilizer (e.g., 1/10 strength) very sparingly, perhaps once every few months during active growth.
