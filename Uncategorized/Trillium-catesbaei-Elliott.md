## Introduction
Trillium (trillium, wakerobin, toadshade, tri flower, birthroot, birthwort, and sometimes "wood lily") is a genus of about fifty flowering plant species in the family Melanthiaceae. Trillium species are native to temperate regions of North America and Asia, with the greatest diversity of species found in the southern Appalachian Mountains in the southeastern United States.

## Care Guide
Bashful Wakerobin thrives in conditions mimicking its native temperate deciduous forest understory. Provide partial to full shade and ensure consistent moisture, especially in spring. Plant in naturally rich, well-draining soil high in organic matter for best results.

### Fertilizer
Prefers naturally rich, well-draining soil high in organic matter. A light annual application of compost or a balanced, slow-release organic fertilizer in early spring can be beneficial, but heavy feeding is generally not needed.
