## Introduction
Arnoglossum atriplicifolium, the pale Indian plantain, is a perennial herbaceous wildflower in the sunflower family (Asteraceae). native to the central and eastern United States. It can reach heights of up to 3 metres (10 ft), with dramatic clusters of white flowers at the top of a central, unbranching stalk.

## Care Guide
Pale Indian Plantain thrives in full sun to partial shade with moderate, consistent watering that imitates the regular rainfall of its native temperate habitats. It prefers average to rich, well-draining soil and typically requires only light fertilization. Ensure good air circulation and avoid waterlogged conditions for optimal growth.
