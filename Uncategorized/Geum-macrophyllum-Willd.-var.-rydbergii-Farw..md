## Introduction
Geum macrophyllum, commonly known as largeleaf avens or large-leaved avens is a flowering plant found from the Arctic south to the northern U.S. states, and in the Rocky Mountains and west to the Sierra Nevada in California and as far south as Northwestern Mexico.
It is even more distinctive in fruit than in flower, with spiky spheres of reddish styles. The fruits are a ball of tiny velcro like hooks that catch on clothing and animal hair.

## Care Guide
Native to the moist environments of Western North American temperate forests and meadows, Largeleaf Avens thrives in partial sun and consistently moist, well-drained, organic-rich soil. Ensure consistent watering and provide moderate fertility for best growth, avoiding dry conditions.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; benefits from fertile, organic-rich soil. Supplement with compost or a balanced slow-release fertilizer in spring if soil is poor.
