## Introduction
Ebony is a dense black/brown hardwood, coming from several species in the genus Diospyros, which also includes the persimmon tree. A few Diospyros species, such as macassar and mun ebony, are dense enough to sink in water. Ebony is finely textured and has a mirror finish when polished, making it valuable as an ornamental wood. It is often cited as one of the most expensive woods in the world.

## Care Guide
Ebony thrives in conditions mimicking its native tropical monsoon climate, requiring full sun to partial shade and a distinct wet-dry watering cycle. Ensure generous watering during active growth and significantly reduce it in dormancy. Moderate fertilization during the growing season supports its robust development.

### Fertilizer
Moderate feeding with a balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer or a liquid fertilizer at half strength every 2-4 weeks during the active growing season. Fertilization should be reduced or halted during the dormant period.
