## Introduction
Maianthemum racemosum, the treacleberry, feathery false lily of the valley, false Solomon's seal, Solomon's plume or false spikenard, is a species of flowering plant native to North America. It is a common, widespread plant with numerous common names and synonyms, known from every US state except Hawaii, and from every Canadian province and territory (except Nunavut and the Yukon), as well as from Mexico.

## Care Guide
This woodland native thrives in partial to full shade with consistently moist, organically rich, and well-draining soil. Protect it from intense afternoon sun and ensure adequate moisture levels, particularly during dry periods, to mimic its natural forest floor habitat.

### Fertilizer
Benefits from rich, organic soil. An annual top-dressing of compost or a balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer in early spring can support vigorous growth.
