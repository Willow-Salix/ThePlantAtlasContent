Rubus flagellaris is a North American species of dewberry in the genus Rubus, a member of the rose family. It is commonly known as northern dewberry and common dewberry. It is widely distributed across the southern and eastern United States into adjacent Canada.

## Care Guide
Northern Dewberry, native to temperate Eastern North America, thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers consistently moist, well-drained soil and benefits from moderate watering, especially during dry spells. Low to moderate fertilization, such as an annual spring application of a balanced fertilizer or compost, supports healthy growth and fruiting.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; an annual application of a balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer in spring, or enrich soil with compost.
