## ðŸŒ± Overview
Pinus albicaulis, known by the common names whitebark pine, white bark pine, white pine, pitch pine, scrub pine, and creeping pine, is a conifer tree native to the mountains of the western United States and Canada, specifically subalpine areas of the Sierra Nevada, Cascade Range, Pacific Coast Ranges, Rocky Mountains, and Ruby Mountains. It shares the common name "creeping pine" with several other plants.
The whitebark pine is typically the highest-elevation pine tree found in these mountain ranges and often marks the tree line. Thus, it is often found as krummholz, trees growing close to the ground that have been dwarfed by exposure. In more favorable conditions, the trees may grow to 29 meters (95 ft) in height.

Whitebark pine thrives in full sun and very well-drained, nutrient-poor soil, reflective of its harsh native subalpine and alpine environments. Consistent moisture is crucial for young plants, but mature trees are drought-tolerant and sensitive to waterlogging. Fertilization is generally unnecessary and best avoided.
