## Introduction
Dimelaena is a genus of lichenized fungi in the family Caliciaceae. Members of the genus are commonly called mountain lichens, or moonglow lichens. They are placodioid crustose lichens, ranging in form from rimose to areolate. The genus has a widespread distribution and contains eight species.

## Care Guide
This arctic-alpine lichen thrives in high sun exposure with cold temperatures and strong winds. It requires infrequent but thorough rehydration, followed by complete drying, as it is poikilohydric. Nutrient needs are met by the environment, making additional fertilization unnecessary.
