## Introduction
The genus Fagopyrum is in the flowering plant family Polygonaceae. It includes some important food plants, such as F. esculentum (buckwheat) and F. tataricum (Tartary buckwheat). The genus is native to the Indian subcontinent, much of Indochina, central and southeastern China, and  central and eastern tropical Africa. Species have been widely introduced elsewhere, throughout the Holarctic and parts of Africa and South America.

## Care Guide
Common Buckwheat is a hardy, fast-growing annual originating from temperate mountainous areas. It thrives in full to partial sun with consistent moisture in well-draining soil. It has low fertilizer requirements and is often grown as a cover crop or for its seeds.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate nutrient needs. It thrives in less fertile soils and can even improve soil quality; avoid high nitrogen fertilizers which promote leaf growth over grain.
