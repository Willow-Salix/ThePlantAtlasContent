## Introduction
Acmispon glaber (previously Lotus scoparius) (common deerweed, deer weed, deervetch, California broom or western bird's-foot trefoil) is a perennial subshrub in the family Fabaceae (pea family). The plant is a pioneer species found in dry areas of California, Arizona, and Mexico.  It is commonly found in many areas including chaparral, coastal sand and roadsides at elevations below 1500 m.

## Care Guide
Common deerweed is a highly adaptable, low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun and dry conditions. It is extremely drought-tolerant, requiring infrequent watering, and performs best in poor, well-draining soils without the need for regular fertilization. This makes it ideal for xeriscaping or arid garden environments.

### Fertilizer
Low to no fertilizer. This plant thrives in lean soils and, as a legume, can fix its own nitrogen.
