## Care Guide
This temperate plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil with moderate, consistent moisture. Once established, it is relatively low-maintenance, requiring infrequent fertilization and tolerating periods of drought. Ensure good air circulation to prevent common hawthorn diseases.

### Fertilizer
Generally low to moderate. In its native habitat, nutrients are recycled from leaf litter. A balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer can be applied in early spring if soil fertility is low or growth is sluggish, but often not necessary for healthy, established plants.
