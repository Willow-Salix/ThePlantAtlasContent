## Introduction
Larix decidua, the European larch, is a species of larch native to the mountains of central Europe, in the Alps and Carpathian Mountains, with small disjunct lowland populations in northern Poland. Its life span has been confirmed to be close to 1000 years, with ages of around 2000 years likely.

## Care Guide
The European Larch thrives in full sun and consistently moist, well-drained soil, replicating its native mountainous environment. It benefits from minimal fertilization, with a balanced, slow-release feed in spring supporting its robust growth as a cold-hardy conifer.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. A balanced, slow-release fertilizer can be applied once annually in early spring, particularly in poor soils, to support healthy growth.
