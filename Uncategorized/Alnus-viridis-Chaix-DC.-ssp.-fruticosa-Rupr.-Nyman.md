## Introduction
Alders are trees of the genus Alnus in the birch family Betulaceae. The genus includes about 35 species of monoecious trees and shrubs, a few reaching a large size, distributed throughout the north temperate zone with a few species extending into Central America, as well as the northern and southern Andes.

## Care Guide
This hardy, nitrogen-fixing shrub thrives in cool, moist conditions, requiring full sun to partial shade and consistently moist, well-drained soil. Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it has low to moderate fertilizer needs. It's well-suited for cooler climates, riverbanks, or open, disturbed sites.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; as a nitrogen-fixer, it can improve soil fertility and typically does not require high nitrogen fertilization
