Tickseed (also tick-seed and tick seed) is a common name for seeds of several plants that stick to fur, clothing, or other surfaces when the plant is brushed against. The term may refer to any such seed in general, but more specifically to:

This perennial thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, characteristic of its native humid subtropical coastal plains. It requires moderate, consistent moisture, allowing the soil to dry slightly between waterings, and tolerates some drought once established. Minimal fertilization is needed, as rich soil can lead to leggy growth.
