## Introduction
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

## Care Guide
The Wet-soil Lupine thrives in consistently moist to wet soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, mirroring its native Californian wet meadow and open woodland habitats. It requires regular and ample watering to prevent drying out. Being a nitrogen-fixing plant, it has minimal fertilizer needs, especially avoiding high-nitrogen formulations.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate fertilization. As a nitrogen-fixing legume, avoid high-nitrogen fertilizers. A balanced, slow-release fertilizer or compost can be applied sparingly once a year if soil nutrients are poor.
