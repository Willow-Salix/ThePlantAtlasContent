## Introduction
Corymbia citriodora, commonly known as lemon-scented gum and other common names, is a species of tall tree that is endemic to north-eastern Australia. It has smooth white to pink bark, narrow lance-shaped to curved adult leaves, flower buds in groups of three, white flowers and urn-shaped or barrel-shaped fruit.

## Care Guide
This tree thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, tolerating various conditions once established. Provide regular water for young plants, reducing frequency as it matures, and apply minimal, low-phosphorus fertilizer. It requires ample space due to its large size.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate fertilization. Native soils in Australia are often low in phosphorus; use a slow-release, low-phosphorus fertilizer if needed, primarily for young trees.
