## Introduction
Juniperus horizontalis, the creeping juniper or creeping cedar, is a low-growing shrubby juniper native to northern North America, throughout most of Canada from Yukon east to Newfoundland, and in some of the northern United States.

## Care Guide
Creeping Juniper thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, reflecting its native habitat on open, rocky, or sandy sites. It is highly drought-tolerant once established and requires minimal fertilization, making it a low-maintenance plant.

### Fertilizer
Low; generally does not require much fertilization. A light application of a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in spring is sufficient for young plants if growth is slow, but established plants rarely need it.
