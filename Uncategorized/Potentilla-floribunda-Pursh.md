## Introduction
Dasiphora fruticosa is a species of hardy deciduous flowering shrub in the family Rosaceae, native to the cool temperate and subarctic regions of the northern hemisphere, often growing at high altitudes in mountains. Dasiphora fruticosa is still widely referenced in the horticultural literature under its synonym Potentilla fruticosa. Common names include shrubby cinquefoil, golden hardhack, bush cinquefoil, shrubby five-finger, widdy, kuril tea and tundra rose.

## Care Guide
This highly adaptable plant thrives in cooler, cold temperate to subarctic climates and prefers full sun to partial shade with consistently moist, well-drained soil. It requires moderate watering to prevent drying out, particularly during establishment, and minimal fertilization, making it a resilient and low-maintenance garden shrub.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. Thrives in a variety of soils, including lean and rocky types, indicating it is not a heavy feeder. A light application of a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in spring, or enrichment with compost, is usually sufficient.
