## Care Guide
Appalachian sedge thrives in partial to full shade, mimicking its native woodland understory. It requires moderate watering to maintain consistently moist, well-draining soil, tolerating brief dry periods once established. Minimal fertilization is needed, as it prefers nutrient levels typical of forest floors.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate fertilizer. Benefits from a light application of a balanced, slow-release granular fertilizer in spring, or amendments with compost to mimic natural woodland soil.
