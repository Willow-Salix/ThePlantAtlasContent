## Introduction
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

## Care Guide
Silver Lupine thrives in full sun and is highly drought-tolerant once established, preferring well-draining soil. As a nitrogen-fixer, it needs minimal to no fertilization, making it a low-maintenance plant. Mimic its native Mediterranean climate by providing ample sunlight and infrequent watering.

### Fertilizer
Generally does not require fertilizer due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. If necessary, a low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus/potassium fertilizer can be applied sparingly in poor soils, but over-fertilization can be detrimental.
