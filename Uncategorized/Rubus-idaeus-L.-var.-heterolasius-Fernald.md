## Introduction
Rubus idaeus (raspberry, also called red raspberry or occasionally European red raspberry to distinguish it from other raspberry species) is a red-fruited species of Rubus native to Europe and northern Asia and commonly cultivated in other temperate regions.

## Care Guide
Red Raspberries thrive in temperate climates, preferring full sun to partial shade and consistently moist, well-drained soil. Mimic their native forest edge habitat by providing regular watering, especially during dry spells, and moderate feeding with organic matter or a balanced fertilizer in early spring for optimal growth and fruit production.

### Fertilizer
Moderate feeding. Amend soil with compost or well-rotted manure annually in early spring. Alternatively, use a balanced slow-release fertilizer formulated for berries.
