## Introduction
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

## Care Guide
Chalk Lupine thrives in full sun and well-draining, often calcareous soils, mimicking its native Mediterranean chaparral environment. It is highly drought-tolerant once established and, being a nitrogen-fixing plant, requires minimal to no fertilization. Focus on ample sunlight and excellent drainage for best results.

### Fertilizer
As a nitrogen-fixing legume, it generally requires no supplemental nitrogen fertilizer. If soil is exceptionally poor, a low-phosphorus, low-nitrogen fertilizer can be applied very sparingly, but is often unnecessary.
