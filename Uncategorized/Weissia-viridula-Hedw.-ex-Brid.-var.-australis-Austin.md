## Introduction
Weissia is a genus of mosses, belonging to the family Pottiaceae.
The genus was described in 1801 by Johann Hedwig and the latest additions to the genus were in 1995 when Weissia ovatifolia and Weissia patula were added. As of July 2021, the genus contains 91 species.

## Care Guide
This widely distributed moss thrives in consistently moist, humid conditions with partial to indirect light. It requires very little fertilization and is tolerant of occasional drying, quickly rehydrating with moisture.

### Fertilizer
Very low. Mosses absorb nutrients from water, dust, and decaying organic matter. A highly diluted (e.g., 1/10 strength) balanced liquid fertilizer can be applied sparingly, or none at all.
