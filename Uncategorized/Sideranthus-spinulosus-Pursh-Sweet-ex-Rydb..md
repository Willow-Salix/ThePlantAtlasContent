## Introduction
Xanthisma, common name sleepy-daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.
The generic name comes from the Greek "xanthos," "yellow", and "-ismos," "condition." It was first described in 1836 from material collected in "The Mexican Province of Texas."

## Care Guide
A tough prairie native, 'Prairie Fan' thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it highly drought-tolerant once established. It requires minimal watering and fertilization, adapting well to lean conditions. Prioritize good drainage and direct sunlight for optimal growth.

### Fertilizer
Low. Generally, no fertilizer is needed in average garden soil. If growth is poor in very lean soil, a light application of balanced granular fertilizer in spring can be given.
