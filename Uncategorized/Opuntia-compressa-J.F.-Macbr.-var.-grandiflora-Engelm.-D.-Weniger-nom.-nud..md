## Introduction
Opuntia humifusa, commonly known as the devil's-tongue, eastern prickly pear or Indian fig, is a cactus of the genus Opuntia present in parts of the eastern United States, southeastern Canada, and northeastern Mexico.

## Care Guide
This temperate prickly pear thrives in full sun and requires very little water, adapting to its native dry grassland environment. Ensure well-draining soil and minimal fertilization to mimic its natural conditions, allowing it to withstand both heat and cold.

### Fertilizer
Low to no fertilizer needed, as it naturally grows in lean soils. If desired, a diluted cactus/succulent formula can be applied once or twice during the active growing season.
