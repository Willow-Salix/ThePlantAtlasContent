## Introduction
Quercus berberidifolia, the California scrub oak, is a small evergreen or semi-evergreen shrubby oak in the white oak section of Quercus. It is native to California and common in  chaparral.

## Care Guide
The California Scrub Oak is a highly drought-tolerant plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, mirroring its native chaparral habitat. Once established, it requires very little water and no additional fertilizer, adapting perfectly to arid, lean conditions.

### Fertilizer
Very low to none. This plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils typical of its native habitat, and excessive fertilization can be harmful.
