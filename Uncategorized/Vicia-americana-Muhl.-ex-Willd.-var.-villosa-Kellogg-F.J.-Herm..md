## Introduction
Vicia americana is a species of legume in the vetch genus known by the common names American vetch and purple vetch. It includes a subspecies known as mat vetch.

## Care Guide
This adaptable North American native thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers consistently moist, well-drained soil. As a nitrogen-fixing legume, it requires minimal to no supplemental fertilization, contributing to improved soil health.

### Fertilizer
Low to no fertilizer needed. As a legume, it fixes its own nitrogen, enriching the soil. Supplemental fertilizer is generally unnecessary.
