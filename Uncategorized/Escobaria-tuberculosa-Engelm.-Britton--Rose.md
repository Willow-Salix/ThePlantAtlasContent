## Introduction
Pelecyphora, pincushion cactus or foxtail cactus is a genus of cacti, comprising 20 species. They originate from Mexico and the United States.
Common species include the Missouri foxtail cactus P. missouriensis, widespread in grassland and forest west of the Mississippi, and the spinystar P. vivipara, distributed across the US and into Canada, first described by Nuttall in 1813.

## Care Guide
This desert cactus demands abundant direct sunlight and extremely well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Water sparingly, only when the soil is thoroughly dry, and provide minimal fertilization. Ensure a dry dormancy period in winter.

### Fertilizer
Minimal; a diluted, low-nitrogen fertilizer formulated for cacti and succulents can be applied once or twice during the active growing season (spring to early summer).
