## Care Guide
This adaptable North American native thrives in full to partial sun, reflecting its preference for open, bright environments as a pioneer species. It requires consistently moist, well-drained soil, especially during establishment, but is moderately drought-tolerant once mature. Fertilization is typically minimal, as it tolerates less fertile soils.

### Fertilizer
Generally not heavy feeders. A light application of a balanced granular fertilizer in early spring may benefit young trees or those in particularly poor soil, but it is often not strictly required in average fertile garden soil.
