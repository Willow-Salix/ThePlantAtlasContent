## Introduction
Eriogonum wrightii is a species of wild buckwheat known by the common names bastardsage and Wright's buckwheat. It is native to the Southwestern United States, California, and northwest Mexico, where it grows in many plant communities, such as chaparral, in rocky habitats from mountains to deserts.

## Care Guide
Bastardsage thrives in full sun and very well-drained, lean soil, mirroring its native arid scrubland and desert habitat. Water infrequently, allowing the soil to dry out completely, as it is highly drought-tolerant. Avoid rich fertilizers, as it is adapted to low-nutrient conditions.
