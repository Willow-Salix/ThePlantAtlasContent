## Introduction
Thatch palm is a common name for several different species of palm trees that are used for thatching, and may refer to:

Coccothrinax, many species native to the Caribbean
Howea, two species native to Lord Howe Island, Australia
Thrinax, three species native to the Caribbean
Cocos nucifera, the coconut tree, used in Makuti thatching

## Care Guide
Thatch Palms thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, tolerating drought once established. Water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out between applications, and fertilize lightly during the growing season to support healthy growth in conditions mimicking its tropical, often exposed, native habitats.

### Fertilizer
Light to moderate feeding. Apply a balanced palm fertilizer 2-3 times during the growing season, especially if grown in nutrient-poor soils like its native sandy or limestone-rich areas.
