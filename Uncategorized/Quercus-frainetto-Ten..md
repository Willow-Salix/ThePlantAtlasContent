## Introduction
Quercus pubescens (synonyms virgiliana), commonly known as the downy oak, pubescent oak or Italian oak, is a species of white oak (genus Quercus sect. Quercus) native to southern Europe and southwest Asia. It is found from northern Spain (Pyrenees) and France in the West to Turkey and the Caucasus in the East.

## Care Guide
The Italian Oak thrives in full sun, reflecting its natural habitat in open woodlands. Once established, it is remarkably drought-tolerant, requiring minimal supplemental watering beyond natural rainfall. Fertilization is generally low-demand for mature specimens, focusing more on healthy soil structure.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. Established trees generally do not require regular fertilization in suitable soils. Young trees may benefit from a balanced, slow-release fertilizer during the growing season to aid establishment.
