## Introduction
Celtis tenuifolia, the dwarf hackberry or Georgia hackberry, is a shrub or small tree 2-to-12-meter-high (6.6 to 39.4 ft). It is native to eastern North America but is very uncommon north of the Ohio River. In Canada, dwarf hackberry is designated as threatened and protected under Canada's Species at Risk Act.

## Care Guide
To care for Dwarf Hackberry, provide full sun to partial shade and ensure well-draining soil. It is very drought-tolerant, so water sparingly once established. This plant prefers lean soils and requires minimal fertilization.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; thrives in lean to average soils and typically does not require heavy fertilization. An annual light feeding in spring may be beneficial if growth is poor.
