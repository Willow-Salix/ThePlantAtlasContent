## Introduction
Oenothera cespitosa, known commonly as tufted evening-primrose or fragrant evening-primrose, is a perennial plant of the genus Oenothera native to western North America from the western edge of the Great Plains and all parts of the Intermountain West in a wide range of dry habitats. Its flowers are open at night and fade by the next day. The species is cultivated in gardens, particularly in its native range.

## Care Guide
This resilient desert native thrives on neglect, preferring full sun and well-draining soil. Water infrequently, allowing the soil to dry thoroughly between applications, as it is highly drought-tolerant. Minimal to no fertilization is required, as it is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

### Fertilizer
Low; generally not needed. If soil is very poor, a light, balanced fertilizer once in spring may be beneficial.
