## Introduction
Saxifraga is the largest genus in the family Saxifragaceae, containing about 473 species of holarctic perennial plants, known as saxifrages or rockfoils. The Latin word saxifraga means literally "stone-breaker", from Latin saxum ("rock" or "stone") + frangere ("to break"). It is usually thought to indicate a medicinal use for treatment of urinary calculi (known as kidney or bladder stones), rather than breaking rocks apart.

## Care Guide
Western Saxifrage thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained conditions, typical of its native subalpine and alpine meadows. Provide bright indirect to partial sun, consistent moisture without waterlogging, and light fertilization during the growing season. Ensure good drainage to mimic its rocky, mountainous habitat and prevent root rot.

### Fertilizer
Light feeding. A balanced, dilute fertilizer once or twice during the growing season is sufficient, or incorporate organic matter into the soil to provide slow-release nutrients.
