## Introduction
Carex hoodii is a species of sedge known by the common name Hood's sedge. It is native to western North America from Alaska to Nunavut to California to South Dakota, where it grows in dry to moist habitat in forests and on mountain slopes.

## Care Guide
Hood's sedge thrives in full sun to partial shade with consistently moist, well-draining soil, showing tolerance for some drought once established. It requires low to moderate fertilization, preferring a light feeding in spring if needed. Mimicking its native montane habitat will ensure robust growth.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. A light application of a balanced slow-release fertilizer in early spring is sufficient, or none if soil is already rich.
