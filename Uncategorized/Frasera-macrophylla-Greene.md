## Introduction
Frasera speciosa is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family (Gentianaceae) known by the common names elkweed, monument plant, and green gentian. When blooming it grows a tall stalk with numerous flowers that have purple dotted green petals. Each plant can flower just once before it dies.

## Care Guide
Elkweed thrives in full to partial sun and requires well-draining soil, mimicking its native montane and meadow habitats. Provide moderate watering, allowing the soil to dry between sessions, and avoid over-fertilization as it prefers leaner conditions. Good drainage is essential to prevent root issues.

### Fertilizer
Low fertilizer needs. These plants are often found in naturally lean to moderately fertile soils. A light, balanced fertilizer application in spring, if desired, is sufficient, but often not necessary.
