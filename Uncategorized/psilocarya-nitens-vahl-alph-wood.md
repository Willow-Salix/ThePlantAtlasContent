The genus includes both annual and perennial species, mostly with erect 3-sided stems and 3-ranked leaves.  The achenes bear a beak-like tubercule (hence the name â€œbeak-rushâ€, although the plants are sedges, not rushes) and are sometimes subtended by bristles.
