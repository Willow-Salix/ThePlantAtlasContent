## Introduction
Luzula campestris, commonly known as field wood-rush or Good Friday grass  is a flowering plant in the rush family Juncaceae. It is also one of the plants known as chimney sweeps  or sweep's broom because of the brush-like appearance of their flowers. This is a very common plant throughout temperate Europe extending to the Caucasus. This species of Luzula is found on all types of native grasslands, and cultivated areas such as lawns, golf-course greens and fields.

## Care Guide
This alpine variety of Field Wood-rush flourishes in cool, bright conditions found in mountain grasslands. It requires consistently moist, well-drained soil and prefers ample light from full sun to partial shade. Fertilization should be minimal, reflecting its adaptation to less fertile native environments.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate fertilization. Native mountain soils are often not exceptionally rich, so a light application of a balanced fertilizer once annually in spring is typically sufficient, or none if grown in good quality soil.
