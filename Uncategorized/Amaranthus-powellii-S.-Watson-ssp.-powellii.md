## Introduction
Amaranthus powellii is a species of amaranth known by the common names Powell's amaranth and green amaranth.
It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, but it is common throughout most of the rest of the temperate Americas as a naturalized species. 
It has also been introduced to other continents, including Australia and Europe.

## Care Guide
Powell's amaranth is a robust annual that thrives in full sun and average, well-draining soil. Provide moderate, consistent watering, allowing the soil to dry slightly between sessions, and light fertilization for optimal growth, mirroring its preference for open, moderately fertile environments.

### Fertilizer
Light to moderate fertilization. It can grow in average soils but benefits from a balanced, all-purpose fertilizer during its active growth, especially in nutrient-poor conditions.
