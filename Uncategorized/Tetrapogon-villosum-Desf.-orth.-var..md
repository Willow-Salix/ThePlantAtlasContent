## Introduction
Tetrapogon is a genus of grasses.
The name Tetrapogon derives from the Greek roots tetra- and pogon, meaning "four" and "beard", respectively, in reference to the tufts of hairs on the plant. The species in this genus are distributed across Africa and through the Middle East as far east as India.

## Care Guide
This drought-tolerant grass thrives in full sun and nutrient-poor, dry soils, characteristic of its native arid and semi-arid habitats. It requires very infrequent watering, allowing the soil to dry out completely between sessions, and generally no fertilizer. Mimicking its desert environment by providing ample sunlight and sparse watering is key to its cultivation.
