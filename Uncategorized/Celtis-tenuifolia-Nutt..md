## Introduction
Celtis tenuifolia, the dwarf hackberry or Georgia hackberry, is a shrub or small tree 2-to-12-meter-high (6.6 to 39.4 ft). It is native to eastern North America but is very uncommon north of the Ohio River. In Canada, dwarf hackberry is designated as threatened and protected under Canada's Species at Risk Act.

## Care Guide
The Dwarf Hackberry is a resilient plant native to temperate woodlands and prairies, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. It is very drought-tolerant once established, requiring minimal watering and fertilizer. This plant is well-suited for low-maintenance landscaping in suitable climates.

Low to moderate; thrives in average to poor, rocky or sandy soils and generally does not require heavy fertilization.
