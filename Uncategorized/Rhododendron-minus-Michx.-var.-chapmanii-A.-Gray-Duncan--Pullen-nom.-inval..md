## Introduction
Rhododendron minus var. chapmanii (syn. Rhododendron chapmanii), also known as Chapman's rhododendron, is an endangered variety, endemic to Florida, of the evergreen Piedmont rhododendron.

## Care Guide
Chapman's Rhododendron thrives in consistently moist, acidic soil under dappled shade, mimicking its native Florida panhandle habitat. Ensure high humidity and provide an acid-specific fertilizer sparingly for optimal health, protecting it from intense direct sun.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate; use an acid-specific fertilizer sparingly in early spring. Avoid high phosphorus formulas.
