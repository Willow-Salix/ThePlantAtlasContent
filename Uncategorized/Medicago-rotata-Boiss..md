## Introduction
Medicago rotata is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. It is found throughout the eastern Mediterranean from Turkey to Israel. It forms a symbiotic relationship with the bacterium Sinorhizobium medicae, which is capable of nitrogen fixation.

## Care Guide
This Mediterranean plant thrives in full sun and is well-adapted to drought, requiring infrequent watering during its active growth and very little during summer dormancy. It needs minimal fertilization due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, preferring well-drained soil.

### Fertilizer
Low to none. As a legume, it fixes nitrogen and can thrive in relatively poor to moderately fertile soils. If necessary, a balanced, low-nitrogen fertilizer can be applied very sparingly in early spring.
