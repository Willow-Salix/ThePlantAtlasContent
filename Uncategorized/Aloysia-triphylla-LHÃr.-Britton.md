## Introduction
Aloysia citrodora, lemon verbena, is a species of flowering plant in the verbena family Verbenaceae, native to South America. Other common names include lemon beebrush. It was brought to Europe by the Spanish and the Portuguese in the 17th century and cultivated for its oil.

## Care Guide
Lemon Verbena thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, mimicking its dry subtropical origins. It is drought-tolerant once established, requiring infrequent deep watering. Fertilize lightly in spring for best results.

### Fertilizer
Light fertilization is sufficient. Apply a balanced, all-purpose fertilizer once in spring or early summer during the active growing season.
