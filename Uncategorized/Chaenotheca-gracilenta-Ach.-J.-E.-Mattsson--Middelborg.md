## Introduction
Chaenotheca is a genus of lichenized fungi within the family Coniocybaceae. The sexual reproduction structures are a mass of loose ascospores that are enclosed by a cup shaped exciple sitting on top of a tiny stalk, having the appearance of a dressmaker's pin (called a mazaedium), hence the common name pin lichen. Genus members are also commonly called needle lichens. Photobiont partners for Chaenotheca include members of the algae genera Symbiochloris, Trebouxia, Trentepohlia, and Tritostichococcus.

## Care Guide
Chaenotheca gracilenta thrives in the stable, moderately humid environments of temperate and boreal forests. Provide bright, indirect to partial sun and ensure consistently high ambient humidity, allowing it to absorb moisture from mist or rain. No fertilization is needed, as it naturally acquires nutrients from its environment.

### Fertilizer
None. Lichens derive necessary nutrients from the air (dust, aerosols) and their substrate (decaying wood, bark). Additional fertilization is not required and can be detrimental.
