## Introduction
Oenothera elata is a species of Oenothera known by the common name Hooker's evening primrose or tall evening primrose. Subspecies include hookeri, hirsutissima, longisima, jamesii, villosa and elata. It is native to much of western and central North America. The plants are quite tall, especially the hookeri subspecies, native to California, which can reach about 1.8 meters (6 feet) height.  The plants are found along roadsides, in moist meadows, or in woodland, from sea level up to 9,000 ft (2,700 m) in elevation.

## Care Guide
Hooker's Evening Primrose thrives in full sun to partial shade with consistently moist, well-drained soil, reflecting its native habitat in riparian zones and open woodlands of Western North America. It is not a heavy feeder, requiring low to moderate fertilization.
