## Introduction
Solidago canadensis, known as Canada goldenrod or Canadian goldenrod, is an herbaceous perennial plant of the family Asteraceae. It forms colonies of upright growing plants, with many small yellow flowers in a branching inflorescence held above the foliage. It is native to northeastern and north-central North America and is an invasive plant in other parts of the continent and several areas worldwide, including Eurasia.

## Care Guide
Canada Goldenrod is a robust North American native, flourishing in full to partial sun and adaptable to various soil types. It is quite drought-tolerant once established but appreciates moderate watering, with minimal fertilizer needs. This makes it a low-maintenance and versatile plant.

### Fertilizer
Low; thrives in average to poor soils and typically does not require heavy fertilization. A light application of balanced fertilizer in spring is sufficient if growth is poor.
