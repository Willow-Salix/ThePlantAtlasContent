## Introduction
Salix babylonica (Babylon willow or weeping willow; Chinese: 垂柳; pinyin: chuí liǔ) is a species of willow native to dry areas of northern China, Korea, Mongolia, Japan, and Siberia but cultivated for millennia elsewhere in Asia, being traded along the Silk Road to southwest Asia and Europe.

## Care Guide
The Corkscrew Willow thrives in full sun and necessitates consistently moist to wet soil, replicating its native temperate riparian habitat. This fast-growing tree benefits from moderate to high fertilization, particularly in spring, to sustain its vigorous development.
