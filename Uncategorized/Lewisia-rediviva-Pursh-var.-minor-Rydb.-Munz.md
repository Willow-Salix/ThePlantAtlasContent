## Introduction
Bitterroot (Lewisia rediviva) is a small perennial herb in the family Montiaceae. Its specific epithet rediviva ("revived, reborn") refers to its ability to regenerate from dry and seemingly dead roots.
The genus Lewisia was moved in 2009 from the purslane family (Portulacaceae) with adoption of the APG III system, to the family Montiaceae.

## Care Guide
Bitterroot thrives in full sun and excellent drainage, mirroring its arid montane origins. Provide minimal water during its spring growth and almost none during summer dormancy to prevent rot, as it prefers lean, nutrient-poor soils. Avoid overwatering and rich fertilization for optimal health.

### Fertilizer
Very low; prefers lean, nutrient-poor, rocky, or sandy soils. Avoid rich fertilizers, as excessive nutrients can lead to lush, weak growth and increased susceptibility to rot.
