## Introduction
Trillium (trillium, wakerobin, toadshade, tri flower, birthroot, birthwort, and sometimes "wood lily") is a genus of about fifty flowering plant species in the family Melanthiaceae. Trillium species are native to temperate regions of North America and Asia, with the greatest diversity of species found in the southern Appalachian Mountains in the southeastern United States.

## Care Guide
This woodland perennial thrives in dappled to full shade, requiring consistently moist, well-drained, and organically rich soil to mimic its native forest floor environment. Ensure good drainage and provide a light feeding of compost or organic fertilizer in early spring for optimal growth and flowering.

### Fertilizer
Benefits from rich, organically amended soil. An annual top-dressing of compost or a light application of a balanced, slow-release organic fertilizer in early spring is sufficient.
