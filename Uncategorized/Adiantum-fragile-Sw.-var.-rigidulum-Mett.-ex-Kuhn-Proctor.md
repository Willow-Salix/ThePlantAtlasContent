## Introduction
Adiantum (), the maidenhair fern (not to be confused with the similar-looking maidenhair spleenwort fern), is a genus of about 250 species of ferns in the subfamily Vittarioideae of the family Pteridaceae, though some researchers place it in its own family, Adiantaceae. The genus name comes from Greek, meaning "unwetted", referring to the fronds' ability to shed water without becoming wet.

## Care Guide
The Fragile Maidenhair requires a humid environment, consistently moist soil (never soggy), and bright, indirect light to mimic its tropical forest floor origins. Avoid direct sun and ensure adequate humidity to prevent its delicate fronds from drying out.

### Fertilizer
Fertilize lightly during the growing season (spring and summer) with a diluted liquid fertilizer, about once every 4-6 weeks. Ferns are not heavy feeders and can be sensitive to over-fertilization.
