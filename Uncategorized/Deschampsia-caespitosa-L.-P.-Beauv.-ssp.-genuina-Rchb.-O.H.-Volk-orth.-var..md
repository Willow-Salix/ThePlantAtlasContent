## Introduction
Deschampsia cespitosa, commonly known as tufted hairgrass or tussock grass, is a perennial tufted plant in the grass family Poaceae. The distribution of this species is widespread, with it being native to North America, Central Africa, eastern Australia, New Zealand, and Eurasia and being introduced to South Africa, South Australia and South America.

## Care Guide
Tufted Hair-grass flourishes in cool, moist environments, preferring full sun to partial shade. It demands consistently wet soil, tolerating periods of waterlogging, and has low to moderate fertilization needs, with a light annual feeding often sufficient.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. Typically benefits from a light, balanced fertilizer application once a year in spring, especially if grown in poor or nutrient-depleted soils.
