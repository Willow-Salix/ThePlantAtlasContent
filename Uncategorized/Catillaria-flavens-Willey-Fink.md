## Introduction
Catillaria is a genus of crustose lichens in the family Catillariaceae. The genus was circumscribed by Italian lichenologist Abramo Bartolommeo Massalongo in 1852. It is the type genus of Catillariaceae, which was circumscribed by Austrian lichenologist Josef Hafellner in 1984.

## Care Guide
This epiphytic lichen thrives in consistently humid and shaded environments, mimicking its native temperate forest habitat. It requires high ambient humidity and indirect light, absorbing moisture and nutrients directly from the air and rainfall, thus requiring no traditional fertilization.

### Fertilizer
No traditional fertilizer is required; this lichen obtains its nutrients from airborne particles, rainfall, and substances leaching from its host substrate (tree bark).
