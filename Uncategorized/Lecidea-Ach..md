## Introduction
Lecidea is a genus of crustose lichens with a carbon-black ring or outer margin (exciple) around the fruiting body disc (apothecium), usually (or always) found growing on (saxicolous) or in (endolithic) rock. Lichens that have such a black exciple are called lecideine, meaning "like Lecidea, even if they are not in this genus. Members of the genus are commonly called disk lichens or tile lichens.

## Care Guide
Lecidea lichens thrive in cool to temperate conditions with clean air and consistent environmental moisture, such as regular misting or high humidity. They prefer moderate indirect light and should never be fertilized, as they obtain nutrients naturally from their environment and are sensitive to chemical inputs.

### Fertilizer
No fertilizer needed. Lichens are highly sensitive to artificial nutrients and pollutants, obtaining all necessary elements from air, rain, and the substrate they colonize.
