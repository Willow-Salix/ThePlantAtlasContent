## Introduction
Corymbia ficifolia, commonly known as red flowering gum, is a species of small tree that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia. It has rough, fibrous bark on the trunk and branches, egg-shaped to broadly lance-shape adult leaves, flower buds in groups of seven, bright red, pink or orange flowers and urn-shaped fruit. It has a restricted distribution in the wild but is one of the most commonly planted ornamental eucalypts.

## Care Guide
The Red Flowering Gum thrives in full sun within a temperate Mediterranean climate, enduring dry summers. Ensure consistent watering during its youth, transitioning to drought-tolerant practices upon maturity. Minimal to no fertilization is required, especially avoiding high-phosphorus products.

### Fertilizer
Low to no fertilizer requirements. These plants are adapted to nutrient-poor soils; excessive phosphorus can be detrimental. If fertilization is deemed necessary, use a slow-release, low-phosphorus formula specifically for Australian native plants.
