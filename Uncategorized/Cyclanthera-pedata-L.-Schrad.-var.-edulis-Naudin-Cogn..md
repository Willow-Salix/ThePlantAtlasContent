## Introduction
Cyclanthera pedata, known as caigua, is a herbaceous vine grown for its edible fruit, which is predominantly used as a vegetable. It is known from cultivation only, and its use goes back many centuries as evidenced by ancient phytomorphic ceramics from Peru depicting the fruits.

## Care Guide
Caigua flourishes in warm, tropical-like conditions with consistently moist, fertile soil. Provide ample direct sunlight, strong support for its vigorous climbing vines, and regular feeding to ensure prolific fruit production.

### Fertilizer
Requires rich, well-draining soil. Fertilize regularly with a balanced organic fertilizer throughout the growing season, particularly when flowering and fruiting.
