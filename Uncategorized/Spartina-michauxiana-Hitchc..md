## Introduction
Spartina pectinata is a species of cordgrass known as prairie cordgrass, freshwater cordgrass, tall marshgrass, and sloughgrass. It is native to much of North America, including central and eastern Canada and most of the contiguous United States except for the southwestern and southeastern regions. Its distribution extends into Mexico. It is also present on other continents as an introduced species.

## Care Guide
Prairie Cordgrass is a resilient wetland plant that thrives in full sun and requires consistently wet to moist soil, even tolerating temporary flooding. It is generally a low-maintenance grass, needing only low to moderate fertilization to flourish in appropriate environments.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate, as it's adapted to often nutrient-rich wetland soils but can benefit from occasional fertilization in poorer conditions.
