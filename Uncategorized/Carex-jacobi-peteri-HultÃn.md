## Introduction
Carex praegracilis is a species of North American sedge known as clustered field sedge, field sedge, and expressway sedge. Carex praegracilis is cultivated in the specialty horticulture trade as lawn substitute and meadow-like plantings.

## Care Guide
This sedge thrives in consistently wet to saturated soil, mirroring its subarctic wetland origins. Provide full sun exposure and ensure the soil never dries out. It has low to average nutrient needs, requiring minimal fertilization.

### Fertilizer
Low to average fertility. A light application of a balanced, slow-release fertilizer once in spring should suffice, especially in nutrient-poor substrates.
