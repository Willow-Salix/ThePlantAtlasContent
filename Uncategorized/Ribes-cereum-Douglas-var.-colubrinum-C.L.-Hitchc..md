## Introduction
Ribes cereum is a species of currant known by the common names wax currant and squaw currant; the pedicellare variety is known as whisky currant. The species is native to western North America.

## Care Guide
The Wax Currant thrives in dry, sunny to partially shaded conditions with well-drained soil, reflecting its native habitat in Western North America. It is highly drought-tolerant once established and requires minimal fertilization, making it a low-maintenance plant.

### Fertilizer
Low nutrient needs. Generally does not require fertilization; if soil is extremely poor, a light application of balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring may be beneficial.
