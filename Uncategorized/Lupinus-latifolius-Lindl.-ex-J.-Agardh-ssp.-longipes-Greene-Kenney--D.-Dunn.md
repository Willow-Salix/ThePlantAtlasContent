## Introduction
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

## Care Guide
Broadleaf Lupine thrives in temperate montane conditions, requiring full sun to partial shade and consistently moist, well-drained soil to emulate its native habitat. As a nitrogen-fixer, it has low fertilizer needs, making a low-nitrogen feeding optional. Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.

### Fertilizer
As a legume, it fixes nitrogen and generally does not require high nitrogen fertilizers. A balanced, low-nitrogen fertilizer or compost can be applied sparingly in early spring if soil appears very poor, but it often thrives without supplemental feeding.
