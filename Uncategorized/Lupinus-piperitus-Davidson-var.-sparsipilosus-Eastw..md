## Introduction
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

## Care Guide
Pepper Lupine thrives in its native California Mediterranean climate, requiring full sun and well-draining soil. It is drought-tolerant once established, needing minimal watering during the dry summer months. As a legume, it has low fertilizer requirements, particularly avoiding excess nitrogen.

### Fertilizer
Low fertilizer needs; as a legume, it fixes nitrogen, so avoid nitrogen-rich fertilizers which can inhibit flowering. A phosphorus-potassium dominant fertilizer could be used sparingly if needed.
