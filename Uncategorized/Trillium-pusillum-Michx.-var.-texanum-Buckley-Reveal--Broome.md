## Introduction
Trillium (trillium, wakerobin, toadshade, tri flower, birthroot, birthwort, and sometimes "wood lily") is a genus of about fifty flowering plant species in the family Melanthiaceae. Trillium species are native to temperate regions of North America and Asia, with the greatest diversity of species found in the southern Appalachian Mountains in the southeastern United States.

## Care Guide
The Texas Dwarf Trillium requires a shaded, consistently moist environment similar to its native woodland understory. Ensure well-draining, organically rich, acidic soil and protect it from harsh afternoon sun to support its delicate nature.

### Fertilizer
Light fertilization with organic matter or a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring. Prefers naturally rich, acidic woodland soil.
