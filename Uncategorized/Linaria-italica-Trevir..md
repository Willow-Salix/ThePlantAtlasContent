## Introduction
Linaria purpurea or purple toadflax is a purple-flowered plant native to Italy, part of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae). It is sometimes planted in gardens and is also an introduced weed in North America and other parts of Europe.

## Care Guide
Purple Toadflax thrives in full sun and requires well-drained soil, preferring moderate watering during its active growth but tolerating significant drought once established. It needs minimal fertilization, adapting well to the leaner soil conditions typical of its Mediterranean native habitat.

### Fertilizer
Low to moderate. This plant is adapted to leaner soils common in its native region and does not require heavy fertilization. An annual light application of a balanced fertilizer in spring is sufficient if growth appears weak.
