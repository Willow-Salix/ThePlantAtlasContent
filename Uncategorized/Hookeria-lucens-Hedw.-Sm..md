## Introduction
Hookeria is a genus of mainly tropical mosses. It was defined by James Edward Smith in 1808 and named for William Jackson Hooker.
There are nine species recognised in the genus Hookeria, the most broadly distributed being Hookeria lucens and Hookeria acutifolia.

## Care Guide
To cultivate Hookeria Moss, replicate its native humid temperate forest environment by providing constant deep shade or very low indirect light, extremely high humidity, and a consistently moist substrate. Avoid direct sun and ensure good air circulation to prevent mold, while also preventing the moss from ever drying out.

### Fertilizer
Generally requires no fertilizer. Mosses absorb sufficient nutrients from their environment; excessive fertilization can be detrimental.
