## Introduction
Amorphophallus paeoniifolius, the elephant foot yam or whitespot giant arum, is a tropical plant native to Island Southeast Asia. It is cultivated for its edible tubers in Southeast Asia, South Asia, Madagascar, New Guinea, and the Pacific islands. Because of its production potential and popularity as a vegetable in various cuisines, it can be raised as a cash crop.

## Care Guide
The Elephant Foot Yam thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright indirect light, mimicking its tropical forest floor habitat. Maintain consistently moist, well-draining soil during its vigorous growth phase, then greatly reduce watering as it enters dormancy. Regular feeding with a balanced fertilizer will support its large foliage and eventual bloom.

### Fertilizer
Feed regularly with a balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half strength every 2-4 weeks during the active growing period. Reduce or stop fertilization during dormancy.
