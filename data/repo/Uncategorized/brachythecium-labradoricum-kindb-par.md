Brachythecium labradoricum thrives in cool, consistently moist, and high-humidity environments with low to medium indirect light, mirroring its native boreal habitat. Ensure the substrate remains damp and provide high ambient humidity through misting or terrarium conditions, while avoiding direct sun and strong fertilizers.

### ðŸ§ª Fertilizer
Extremely low to no fertilizer is needed. Mosses generally absorb nutrients from their environment (rainwater, decaying matter) and can be harmed by high concentrations of salts found in typical fertilizers. If desired, a highly diluted, balanced liquid fertilizer can be applied very sparingly, perhaps once or twice a year.
