Crocus vernus (spring crocus, giant crocus) is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae, native to the Alps, the Pyrenees, and the Balkans. Its cultivars and those of Crocus flavus (Dutch crocus) are used as ornamental plants. The Dutch crocuses are larger than the other cultivated crocus species (e.g., Crocus chrysanthus). Dutch Crocus prefers a location with full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. Ensure consistent moisture during its spring growing season, then allow to dry out completely as the plant enters summer dormancy. Minimal fertilization in the fall will support robust flowering.

Light feeding is generally sufficient. Incorporate compost or a balanced, slow-release bulb fertilizer (low nitrogen, higher phosphorus and potassium) in the fall, especially in poor soils. Over-fertilization, particularly with high nitrogen, can hinder flowering.
