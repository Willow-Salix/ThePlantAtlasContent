Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. Silver Lupine thrives in full sun and well-drained, even poor, soils, reflecting its native montane and semi-arid origins. It is drought-tolerant once established and should be watered infrequently, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings. Due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, it requires little to no fertilizer, particularly avoiding high-nitrogen formulations.
