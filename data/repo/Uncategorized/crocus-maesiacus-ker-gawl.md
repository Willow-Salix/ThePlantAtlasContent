Crocus vernus (spring crocus, giant crocus) is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae, native to the Alps, the Pyrenees, and the Balkans. Its cultivars and those of Crocus flavus (Dutch crocus) are used as ornamental plants. The Dutch crocuses are larger than the other cultivated crocus species (e.g., Crocus chrysanthus). After blooming, allow foliage to die back naturally and keep the soil relatively dry during its summer dormancy to ensure successful re-blooming next year. Minimal fertilization is needed.
