## ðŸŒ± Overview
Lupinus, commonly known as lupin, lupine, or regionally bluebonnet, is a genus of plants in the legume family Fabaceae. The genus includes over 199 species, with centres of diversity in North and South America. Smaller centres occur in North Africa and the Mediterranean. They are widely cultivated, both as a food source and as ornamental plants, but are invasive to some areas.

This lupine thrives in full sun and lean, well-drained soils, consistent with its Mediterranean and semi-arid native climate. It requires moderate watering during its spring growth period but is highly drought-tolerant once established, preferring dry summer conditions. Avoid over-fertilization, especially with nitrogen, due to its natural nitrogen-fixing ability.
